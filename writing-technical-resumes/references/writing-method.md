# Writing method — task list → results showcase

A three-step rewrite plus an example library. Learn the *form* of the examples, not their content.

## Three steps (per experience)

1. **Context + your contribution (1 sentence)** — what it was, and what you delivered in it.
   Don't pile up jargon.
2. **Action (skillset + keywords)** — the concrete techniques/methods used, one concrete move per
   clause, naturally embedding role keywords (helps both ATS and human readers).
3. **Result (facts / data / numbers)** — quantified results only; if you have no hard number, give
   scale/scope (cases handled, nodes, data volume, times reused, published).

**Self-test:** read the "result" clause alone. If it sounds like a process ("turned the flow into
a tool") rather than a result ("18/18 nodes, 0 missing"), rewrite it.

## Key principles

- **Outcomes, not tasks:** not "I did task X", but "doing X produced what".
- **Make the role concrete:** in the entry header give a descriptive role ("independently designed
  and built", "author/maintainer"), not just a project name.
- **Quantify with symbols that catch the eye:** `%`, raw numbers, `N items/steps`, version numbers.
- **Relevance first:** the experience closest to the target role gets the most space and the top spot.

## Example library (copy the structure, not the story)

These bullets from senior templates are models — study their shape:

| Example (template original) | The form you learn |
|---|---|
| `Created and supervised team of 25 including 11 engineers; produced 14 projects to date, accounting for $13M revenue` | One bullet, three quantification layers: scale (25/11) → output (14) → value ($13M), with a semicolon joining "what I did" and "what it produced" |
| `Coached team through 15-month cycle; ultimately captured 5% of $130M market` | `ultimately + hardest result` at the sentence end for impact |
| `Increased sales 500% in two years` | Dare to use a **pure-metric short bullet** on its own |
| `Developed 'C-Tech' patented system — licensed to global manufacturer` | **Named artifact + external adoption** (third-party validation) |

Worked rewrite (technical):
- Weak: "Demonstrates engineering fundamentals in CLI parsing and algorithm implementation."
  (filler verb + no result)
- Strong: "All 4 samples run with test coverage and a one-command build; all pass a single
  lint/typecheck/test/build gate." (no filler + quantified + lands on a verifiable artifact)

## "List then tailor" — or "write everything, then cut"

The classic method is "list all experiences → tailor variants per role". A lighter variant for a
single target: **write every project/paper into one resume (let it run to 2 pages) → cut to 1 page
by relevance.** Don't pre-split into multiple role versions unless explicitly required.
